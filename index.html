About

I am an applied microeconomist specializing in health economics and labor economics. My research investigates the causal effects of public policies—health insurance expansions, labor market regulations, and pandemic responses—on individual well-being, health behaviors, and labor market outcomes.


Methodologically, I employ quasi-experimental designs including difference-in-differences, instrumental variable estimation, and regression discontinuity to establish credible causal inference from observational data. My work spans the intersection of health and labor, with particular attention to how social connectedness, insurance coverage, and workplace arrangements shape population health and economic outcomes.


Prior to joining Dongguk University, I served as a Research Fellow at the Korea Insurance Research Institute (KIRI) and as an Associate Professor at Gachon University. I received my Ph.D. in Economics from the University at Albany, SUNY.

Does Legalization of Sunday Alcohol Sales Increase Crime? Evidence from Seven States
Baris K. Yörük and Jungtaek Lee
Journal of Studies on Alcohol and Drugs, 2018, Vol. 79(6), pp. 816–825 SSCI
